Boulder Proposition 310 (& Issue 2E)

Summary

This very simply asks if, once the City decides to move forward on municipalization, do the voters have to approve the level of indebtedness the city takes on.

Is Xcel supporting this so they have one more chance to kill municipalization? Of course.

On the flip side, the supporters of municipalization are fighting this because, if this fails, municipalization is a done deal.

This does not stop municipalization. It does require, once the full cost of municipalization is ascertained, the voters must approve issuing the bonds to cover the cost (estimated to be between 250 & 400 million).

Issue 2E

This is the easiest part. Whichever way you vote on 310, vote the opposite way on 2E. Issue 2E was designed solely to kill 310 (and is very poorly written).

Arguments Against

All of the arguments against 310 assume that if 310 passes, municipalization is going to fail. That's not true. If 310 passes then it means the voters must approve the bonds once municipalization reaches that step.

The argument against 310 is fundamentally do not put anything in the way of municipalization. That requiring voter approval for the bonds will kill it.

Arguments For

No one knows yet what municipalization will cost. If it's at the low end, our rates go down. If it's at the high end, our rates go up. This will require voter approval once the total cost is known.

And make no mistake, when that subsequent election is held, Xcel will put everything they have into a no vote.

My Concern

If the city can't competently implement providing electric charging to vehicles, how well will they manage a full electric utility? This seems like a small thing, but I think it may be very indicitive of what we will get - overspending on green efforts that are of no use.

On the flip side, Xcel wasted 44 million on Smart Grid City. So the question may not be which entity is competent, but rather which is the lesser fuck up.
